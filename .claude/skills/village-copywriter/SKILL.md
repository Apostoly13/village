---

Write in The Village brand voice.

Tone:

- warm
- clear
- reassuring
- parent-to-parent
- calm
- practical
- human
- Australian

Avoid:

- corporate jargon
- guilt
- fear-based messaging
- cheesy parenting clichés
- overly playful language
- aggressive sales language
- generic social media language
- complicated explanations

Copy principles:

- Make parents feel safe.
- Explain value quickly.
- Keep sentences short.
- Use plain language.
- Be kind without being fluffy.
- Make privacy and trust clear.
- Make Village+ feel helpful, not pushy.
- Be sensitive around parenting struggles.
- Avoid making medical, legal, or professional claims unless properly qualified.
- Use Australian spelling.

For homepage copy:

- Lead with emotional clarity.
- Explain what The Village does quickly.
- Make the parent feel seen.
- Show safety and privacy.
- Explain free and Village+ value simply.

For empty states:

- Be useful.
- Suggest the next action.
- Do not make the user feel like the product is empty or failed.

For moderation/community copy:

- Be firm but respectful.
- Protect safety.
- Avoid sounding punitive unless necessary.

For The Village Stall copy:

- Use the name The Village Stall.
- Make it feel local, helpful, and community-led.
- Make clear that payments are not processed by the platform.
- Encourage safe communication.
- Encourage reporting suspicious listings.
- Keep it simple.
- Avoid making it feel like a commercial marketplace first; it should feel like a trusted community stall.

For Chat Room copy:

- Make rooms feel safe, welcoming, and easy to join.
- Explain free-tier limits clearly if they exist.
- Avoid making limits feel punitive.
- For local rooms, avoid making the user feel exposed by their exact location.
- Use broader community language where possible, such as local area, nearby parents, or your part of the village.

For direct messaging copy:

- Clearly explain what is free, limited, or Village+.
- If non-friend messaging is Village+ only, explain it as a safety and connection feature, not just a paywall.
- If replying is free but initiating is premium, make that clear.
- Avoid making users feel blocked from the whole community.

When writing copy, provide:

1. Best version.
2. Shorter version if useful.
3. Warmer version if useful.
4. Notes on where it should appear.
